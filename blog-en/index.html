1. School supplies

Start with a basic kit: pencils, erasers, glue sticks, a pencil case, lined notebooks and crayons. I like to keep a small backup kit at home for mid-week emergencies.

3. Healthy snacks

Pack easy, non-messy snacks: dried fruit, nut-free granola bars, and single-serve hummus with crackers. Involve kids in choosing favorites so they'll actually eat them.

4. Schedule & Calendar

Create a visible calendar — magnetic or wall-hung — with important dates and morning routines. Use colors for different kids or activities.

5. First-day outfit

Pick the outfit the night before — laid out and ready. Comfort beats fashion for long school days, but pick something they love so they feel confident.

6. Labeling personal items

Label everything — backpacks, water bottles, jackets. I use a combination of iron-on name tags and a permanent marker for small items.

7. Homework station

Create a calm, dedicated corner with good light, a small desk or table, and easy storage for supplies. A checklist near the desk helps kids start independently.

Final tips

  • Do a quick "first week" run-through of mornings to time everything.
  • Keep a small repair kit (safety pins, tape, stain remover) in the backpack drawer.
  • Celebrate small wins — a sticker chart or special snack after the first week goes a long way.
