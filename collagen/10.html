What is Collagen?

Collagen is the most abundant protein in your body, making up about 30% of your total protein content. Think of it as the "glue" that holds your body together - it's found in your skin, bones, muscles, tendons, and blood vessels.

As we age, our natural collagen production decreases by about 1% per year after age 25. This decline leads to wrinkles, joint stiffness, and weaker bones. Supplementing with collagen can help restore what time takes away.
