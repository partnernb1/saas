Can Collagen Reverse The Effects Of Aging?

Discover what collagen really does for your hair, skin, and nails

By Dr. Sarah Mitchell ‚Ä¢ December 15, 2024 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

"Collagen is the most abundant protein in our bodies, making up about 30% of our total protein content. But as we age, our natural collagen production decreases by about 1% per year after age 25."

What Exactly Is Collagen?

Collagen is a structural protein that acts like the scaffolding of our body. Think of it as the glue that holds everything together - from our skin and bones to our tendons and blood vessels. There are at least 16 types of collagen, but about 80-90% of the collagen in our body consists of types I, II, and III.

üß¨ Types of Collagen:

  • Type I: Found in skin, bones, tendons (90% of body's collagen)
  • Type II: Found in cartilage and joints
  • Type III: Found in muscles, organs, and blood vessels

The Science Behind Collagen and Aging

As we age, several factors contribute to collagen breakdown: UV exposure, pollution, smoking, poor diet, and natural aging processes. This leads to visible signs of aging like wrinkles, sagging skin, brittle nails, and thinning hair.

Recent studies have shown promising results for collagen supplementation. A 2019 review published in the Journal of Drugs in Dermatology found that oral collagen supplements can improve skin hydration, elasticity, and reduce signs of aging.
