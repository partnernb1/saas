What Is Collagen?

Collagen is the most abundant protein in our body, making up about 30% of our total protein content. It acts as the "scaffolding" that holds our tissues together, providing structure and strength to our skin, hair, nails, bones, and joints.

Starting around age 25, our natural collagen production decreases by approximately 1% per year. This decline accelerates after menopause and is responsible for many visible signs of aging.
